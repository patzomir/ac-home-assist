# Smart Plug — Installation Guide

> This document is the source for the printed installation insert shipped with the smart plug.
> It must be readable as a standalone paper page — no assumed prior knowledge.

---

## Before You Install — Read This First

The smart plug has the **same 16A rating as a standard Schuko wall socket**.

**If your AC is already plugged into a standard Schuko wall socket — you are good to go.**
The smart plug does not introduce a weaker link. It is a like-for-like replacement with added remote control and energy monitoring.

**If your situation does not match the checklist below — do not install the plug.**
Contact us and we will advise on the correct solution for your setup.

---

## Pre-Installation Checklist

Complete every item before plugging in the smart plug. If any item does not apply, stop and contact support.

- [ ] My AC unit is plugged directly into a **wall socket** — not an extension cord, not a multi-socket adapter, not a power strip
- [ ] The wall socket has a **standard Schuko plug** (round, two pins + grounding clip, rated 16A)
- [ ] The socket and plug are in **good condition** — no visible scorch marks, no loose fit, no heat discoloration
- [ ] My AC unit is an **inverter type** (variable-speed compressor) — not a fixed-speed window or portable unit
- [ ] The AC nameplate shows a rated current of **16A or less** (check the label on the side or back of the unit)

---

## Installation Steps

1. **Turn off the AC** using the remote or its own power button.
2. **Unplug the AC** from the wall socket.
3. **Plug the smart plug into the wall socket** — push firmly until fully seated.
4. **Plug the AC into the smart plug**.
5. **Power on the AC** and confirm it operates normally.
6. Follow the hub setup instructions to connect the smart plug to your system.

---

## What Is Not Covered

The following configurations are **outside the supported installation scope**. Using the smart plug in these situations voids any warranty and transfers full responsibility to the installer:

- Extension cords or multi-socket adapters between the wall and the smart plug
- Sockets or plugs showing signs of wear, heat damage, or loose contact
- Fixed-speed (non-inverter) AC units or compressor-type appliances
- AC units with a rated current above 16A
- Industrial, three-phase, or hardwired AC installations
- Any installation performed by an unqualified person in a context requiring a licensed electrician
